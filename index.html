LORE

They say the knight who clears the Lower Halls of the dungeon will have his name carved into the gates of the capital. Songs will be written. Statues will be raised. Kings will be brought to their knees before thee.

But no one dares ask what happened to the last knight who believed that.

You've heard the stories. The dungeon beneath the ruined keep, older than the crown, hungrier than any war. Every generation, someone goes down swearing they'll be the one who finally clears it. Every generation, the halls go deeper. Get colder. Crowded with skulls.

Tonight, it's your turn.

You tell yourself this is different. You tell yourself you're stronger, smarter, luckier than the ones who came before. The dungeon doesn't argue. It never has to. It just opens the door.

Clear each chamber and make it to the next set of stairs.

Fame awaits you at the bottom. If you can get there.
